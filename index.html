What is Chess?

Chess was first created by Garry Chess in 1975. Some people will try and convince you that chess was actually invented before the 6th century AD in India. That, however, is quite obviously a lie.

Meeting Times

Meet the King

The king is considered priceless, as checkmate is reached when the king is cornered. The king can move in any direction but is limited to one square.

Meet the Queen

The queen is considered to have the highest numerical value of all the chess pieces due to its high functionality. The queen can move in all directions. It has a value of 9.

Meet the Rook

The rook can move vertically and horizontally on the board. It has a value of 5 and comes in handy during a move called 'castling'. Each player has two rooks.

Meet the Bishop

The bishop can move diagonally and has a value of 3. Each player starts of with two.

Meet the Knight

The knight moves in 'L' shaped patterns and is the only piece which can hop over other pieces. It also has a value of 3. Each player begins with two.

Meet the Pawn

Perhaps one of the least appreciated pieces, the pawn can only move one space forward after its first move, in which it can move up to two spaces. The pawn has a value of 1 and each player begins with 8 pawns.
